XPS 15 (2020)

The New XPS 15 Design: A More Modern Facelift

Dell's XPS laptop line is consistently one of the BESTS, and this year's model-9500 update to the flagship 15-inch
version is no exception. The 2020 XPS 15 (starts at $1,299.99; $2,299.99 as tested) boasts a fresh new design with
a trimmer chassis and a little more screen-pixel real estate, as well as the latest parts from Intel and Nvidia.

Dell's wealth of configs means you can outfit this high-quality build with a wide combination of parts and features.

The lid and chassis are made of aluminum, and the keyboard deck is a soft-touch carbon fiber. These give the XPS 15
a quality feel, though it's not the lightest laptop at 4.5 pounds. It definitely feels hefty.

All of this slimming down took place while making the screen larger, taking up more of the laptop's overall size. Super-
thin bezels add up to a 92 percent screen-to-body ratio, and the display now sports a 16:10 aspect ratio with more
vertical pixels than the former 16:9 layout. The higher percentage of display looks more modern, and aesthetically
much more pleasing to all.


The actual display quality is superb. We have the optional 4K-resolution (3,840-by-2,400-pixel) touch panel on our model,
and the screen is incredibly sharp and vibrant. The screen is rated for HDR 400, maxes out at 500 nits of brightness, and
covers 100 percent of the Adobe RGB spectrum and 94 percent of DCI-P3. The XPS 15 panels have looked pretty stunning
for years (even more than usual on the model I tested last year, in an optional OLED-screen configuration), and this is no
different. The alternative option to our panel is a full HD (1,920-by-1,200-pixel) non-touch screen.

Keyboard and Speakers

The size and the screen are not the only physical changes. For one, the new model features up-facing speakers (you can
see them alongside the keyboard in the photos above and below), pushing the sound to the user more directly than the
down-facing speakers of past models.

As far as laptop speakers go, the XPS 15 sounds excellent, and the sound carries farther without having to blast the volume.
Additionally, and following in the footsteps of Apple's MacBooks, the XPS 15's touchpad is now gigantic, making it much
easier to pan and scroll. The keys seem mostly unchanged, comfortable to type with moderate travel & white backlighting.


Port Changes and Configuration Choices

Another big change is in the port options. The last model included both USB Type-C and USB Type-A ports, as well as a
variety of other options, but this version does a lot of streamlining. Other than an SD card slot and a headphone jack,
the only physical connections on this XPS 15 are USB-C ports, with two on the left flank and one more on the right.
The two on the left include Thunderbolt 3 and DisplayPort support

USB-C is fast and convenient, but some may be worried about their USB-A peripherals. Fortunately, Dell smartly
includes both USB-C-to-USB-A and USB-C-to-HDMI adapters in the box.

The updates naturally extend to the internal components. Our $2,299.99 configuration includes a 10th Generation
Intel Core i7-10875H processor, 16GB of memory, a 512GB solid-state drive, an Nvidia GeForce GTX 1650 Ti GPU,
and the 4K touch display. There are some set base models, but with Dell's configuration options, you can essentially
configure whatever combination of components you want within this laptop's wheelhouse.

This includes options for integrated graphics rather than our Nvidia GPU, while other components max out at 64GB
of memory and 2TB of storage. There is no OLED screen option currently, despite its presence on the past model.
One thing to note is that different capacities of battery are tied to the GPU option: A 56WHr battery comes with the
iGPU, and discrete GPU configs get the 86WHr option. Also worth keeping an eye on: Core i9 processors will
be available for the XPS 15

PROS CONS
+ Sleek premium design and beautiful display - A tad hefty for its size
+ Speedy all-around performance - USB Type-C only could prove an obstacle for some users
+ Plenty of configuration options, including capable discrete GPU - GPU falls short of hardcore gamers' requirements
+ Three USB-C ports, two with Thunderbolt 3 support
+ USB Type-A and HDMI adapters included
+ Robust speakers and roomy touchpad
